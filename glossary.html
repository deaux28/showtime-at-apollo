Key Metrics & Terminology
Definitions for every metric, acronym, and calculation used across the dashboards
MTD
Month-to-Date
Revenue or units accumulated from the first calendar day of the current month through the most recent reported date. Used in the KPI strip on the HW Performance Dashboard (e.g., "April MTD Revenue: $3.44M as of April 23").
QTD
Quarter-to-Date
Registrations or revenue accumulated from the first day of the current fiscal quarter through the latest reported week. Q2 2026 begins April 6; QTD registration weeks are Apr 6, Apr 13, and Apr 20 (partial).
OPlan
Operating Plan
The internally committed revenue target set at the start of the fiscal period. OPlan is the primary benchmark for evaluating actual performance. April OPlan = $4.50M; Q2 OPlan = $17.2M.
Distinct from Committed Forecast, which is updated in-quarter by the field.
Committed Forecast
Also: Committed Fcst
A field-updated revenue forecast, typically revised monthly. Reflects the sales team's current expectation for the period. Q2 Committed = $18.0M vs. Q2 OPlan $17.2M (+4.9%).
Booked USD
Booked Revenue (US Dollars)
Revenue recognized from confirmed purchase orders or completed transactions, grouped by product group and territory. Shown in the Booked USD Summary dashboard, sourced from the Omni Hardware Sales & Forecast workbook.
Open Order Backlog
Unfulfilled Orders
The dollar value of orders placed but not yet shipped/fulfilled within the reporting period. April backlog = $1.15M. Backlog can bridge into the next period if fulfilled.
Sell-In
Channel Sell-In / Shipments to Trade
Revenue from units shipped from Universal Audio to distributors or retailers (the trade channel). OPlan is structured as a sell-in target. Compare to Sell-Through.
Sell-In Pace = Monthly OPlan ÷ 4 weeks
Sell-Through
End-Customer Sell-Through / Registrations
Units purchased and activated by end customers (consumers), measured via product registrations. Sell-through can exceed sell-in pace when channel inventory is being depleted.
Registrations
End-User Product Registrations (Units)
Units activated by end customers through the UA registration system. Sourced from fct_registrations. Used as a proxy for sell-through. Weekly registration data begins April 6 (Q2 week 1); the Mar 30 week is Q1 context only.
Partial weeks (e.g., Apr 20 = Mon–Wed only) are flagged with an asterisk (*) in charts.
Reg Dollars (Static ASP)
Registration Dollars using Static Average Selling Price
A revenue proxy calculated by multiplying registration unit counts by a fixed (static) average selling price per SKU. Not actual booked revenue — used to approximate the dollar value of sell-through activity when invoice data isn't available at the SKU level.
OPlan Pace
OPlan Weekly Pace / OPlan Channel Perpetual (Sell-In)
The implied weekly revenue run-rate derived from the monthly OPlan. Used as a benchmark line in the weekly registration dollar charts. Any week where actual Reg Dollars exceeds OPlan Pace is highlighted green.
OPlan Pace = Monthly OPlan ÷ 4 weeks
WOS
Weeks of Supply
A channel inventory health metric. Measures how many weeks of current sell-through velocity is covered by on-hand inventory. Computed over a trailing 13-week window per dealer × product group.
WOS = Avg Weekly On-Hand ÷ Avg Weekly Units Sold
Target channel turns: 1× per quarter = 13 weeks of supply.
Months of Supply
MoS — Dealer-Level Inventory Coverage in Months
Used in the B&H Photo Dealer WOS Report. Equivalent to WOS expressed in months. A supply coverage of ~3 months is considered healthy for a major dealer. Shown in the Tier 1 bar chart and Tier 3 trend line by SKU over 15 months.
ASP
Average Selling Price
The average price at which a product is sold. In the QTD Registration Summary, a "Static ASP" is used — a fixed price per SKU applied uniformly to registration counts to estimate dollar value.
$ Var vs OPlan
Dollar Variance vs. Operating Plan
The difference between Actual Revenue and OPlan for a given product line or time period. Positive = over-plan; Negative = under-plan. Displayed in the Revenue vs. Plan tab of the HW Performance Dashboard.
$ Var = Actual Revenue − OPlan
% of OPlan
Percent of Operating Plan Achieved
Actual revenue expressed as a percentage of the OPlan target. 100% = on plan. The HW Performance KPI strip shows "vs April OPlan Pace: 99.6%" for MTD revenue through April 23.
% of OPlan = Actual ÷ OPlan × 100

Weeks of Supply (WOS) — Status Thresholds
Color-coded inventory health categories applied in Channel Inventory and Dealer WOS dashboards
Status WOS Range Color Code Interpretation & Action
Stockout Risk < 4 weeks Red Channel is nearly out of stock. Replenishment orders should be expedited. Risk of lost sales at retail.
Healthy / Low 4 – 8 weeks Green Optimal inventory level. Enough coverage to bridge a reorder cycle without excess. Target range for most SKUs.
Elevated 8 – 13 weeks Yellow Above the quarter-turn target. Monitor sell-through velocity; hold or reduce incoming orders.
Overstocked 13 – 26 weeks Orange 2× the quarterly turn target. Indicates a demand shortfall or over-shipment. Review promotion options.
Extreme Overstock > 26 weeks Purple More than 6 months of supply. Likely tied to low/no sell-through velocity. May indicate stale or mismapped inventory. Confirm data quality (e.g., Guitar Center double-counting risk).
No Velocity ∞ (zero sales) Gray On-hand quantity exists but no units sold in the trailing 13 weeks. WOS is undefined (division by zero). Displayed as ∞.

⚠ Data quality note: Guitar Center WOS figures may include store-level + HQ double-counting per PRD data quality note. Confirm before action.

Product ID Field Relationship Patterns
The 9 patterns that describe how Product ID, SKU ID, and Core Product ID relate across 137 active records
Full Match 18 records
Product ID = SKU ID = Core Product ID. All three fields are identical. Highest data quality. Common in Analog Mics (MIC-UA187, MIC-UASD-1) and classic Analog hardware (LA2A, 1176LN, 4710D).
Core = PID, SKU Differs 29 records
Core Product ID equals Product ID exactly, but SKU ID is a human-readable alternate form. Common in Volt (VOLT876 → "VOLT-876"), GPM pedals (GPM-DRM → "DREAM-PEDAL"), and GPS pedals (GPS-1176 → "1176-PEDAL").
Core is Prefix of PID 10 records
Core Product ID is the base stem of Product ID (suffix stripped). E.g., Core "APLTWXDU" → PID "APLTWXDU-HE". The suffix encodes a variant tier (HE = Heritage Edition, STU = Studio, etc.).
Shared Core — Sibling Variants 12 records
Multiple Product IDs share one Core Product ID. These are product tier variants (e.g., APX16G2-ESS and APX16G2-ULT both use Core "APX16G2"). Core ID is the correct rollup key for base-model analysis.
Bundle / Subscription 31 records
SKU ID = Product ID, and Core Product ID is intentionally blank. These are software bundles (UAD-SELECT, UAD-COMPLETE4), subscriptions (UAD-SPARK-ANNUAL), and plugin packs. No Core expected — this is by design.
⚠ Descriptive SKU, Missing Core 5 records
Hardware products with long-form descriptive SKUs (UAD-2-APOLLO-...) but no Core Product ID populated. Unlike bundles, these are physical hardware with siblings that DO have Core IDs — the gap is a data entry inconsistency, not by design.
Refurbished — Shared SKU 12 records
Refurb products share their parent's SKU ID but carry a "-REFURB" suffix in Product ID (e.g., LA2A-REFURB shares SKU "LA2A"). Only 6176-REFURB has Core populated. The other 11 refurbs are invisible in Core-level rollups — a silent undercounting risk.
Accessory — No IDs Expected 17 records
Physical accessories (power supplies, cables, mic stands) with no SKU ID or Core Product ID — by design. E.g., PSU-TWIN/X4-US, CBL-SPHERE25, SM-251. Product ID exists for order tracking but no family rollup is needed.
⚠ Missing / No IDs 3 records
Records with blank Product ID (LUNA-UPGRADE: $1,626) or no SKU and no Core (APE1X: $1,361 booked with no IDs). These records cannot be attributed to a product family. Revenue is orphaned in product-level reporting.

Adaptive Insights (AI) Level

"Adaptive Insights Level" (AI Level) is the dealer/account grouping key used in the sell-through registration system. Each AI Level corresponds to a specific dealer or regional distributor group. Used in the QTD Registration Summary's dealer breakdown and in the Bomisco Inventory dashboard (Top 5 AI Accounts = top 5 by on-hand inventory volume). The AI Level label may be a company name (e.g., "Amazon", "Hook Up") or a territory roll-up code (e.g., "EMEA_Dist_Gen").

Primary data warehouse. Powers Booked USD, Revenue vs Plan, and Channel Inventory tabs. Accessible via the Hardware Sales & Forecast Workbook.
